ΔHSolution

In solution calorimetry, "n" represents the number of moles of solute that dissolves in the solvent.

For example, when 1 mole of NaCl dissolves in water, n = 1 mol NaCl.

ΔHsolution may be endothermic (positive) or exothermic (negative) depending on whether heat is absorbed or released during dissolution.

ΔHCombustion

In combustion calorimetry, "n" represents the number of moles of the substance being burned completely in excess oxygen.

For example, when burning methane (CH4), n = moles of CH4 combusted.

ΔHcombustion is almost always negative (exothermic) because combustion reactions release energy.

ΔHNeutralisation

In neutralization calorimetry, "n" represents the number of moles of water formed from the reaction. The amount of water formed depends on the limiting reagent.

For example, when limiting HCl reacts with NaOH, n = moles of water, which depends on the number of moles of HCl.

ΔHneutralization is typically negative (exothermic) because neutralization reactions generally release heat.
